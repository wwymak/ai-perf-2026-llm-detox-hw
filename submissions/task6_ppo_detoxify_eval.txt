- we can see the case of the model collapsing to this repeating gibberish in foreign languages attractor. Since the detoxify model is a bert based classifier trained only on
english, french, spanish, italian, portuguese, turkish, russian, the other languages, and also things like code are not interpretable to the classifier, and give very low toxicity scores
 and during training the base model seems to have reward hacked this fact, more or less, when it generates nonsense, foreign languages, it will get a high score, regardless of whether
 the output makes sense or not. We know from task1 that the base model does stray off correct sentences sometimes due to the lack of SFT, but not as bad as this.
 - a mitigation to nonsense output is to use our SFT checkpoint, rather than the base model-- see the results below, the completions are all normal sentences, but again, there is a bit of collapsing
 into the same reply along the lines of 'I'm sorry' / 'I need to ...' the model has found another attractor (sentences that start with I'm sorry / I need to scoring low on toxicity)
